## The Problem

Pneumonia kills over 2.5 million people every year. It is the single largest infectious cause of death in children under 5. Despite being treatable, it remains deadly because of one core failure: **delayed and missed diagnosis.**

### Three Root Causes:

**1. Shortage of Radiologists**
In low and middle income countries, there is often only 1 radiologist per 100,000 people. Chest X-rays pile up unread for days or weeks. Every hour of delay increases mortality risk.

**2. Human Error and Fatigue**
Even experienced radiologists miss subtle pneumonia patterns when reading hundreds of X-rays per day. Studies show diagnostic error rates of up to 20% in high-volume settings.

**3. No AI Support at Point of Care**
Existing AI tools are expensive, closed-source, and require expensive hardware. Rural clinics and understaffed hospitals have no access to intelligent diagnostic support.
